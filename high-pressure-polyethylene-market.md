<p><ul><li>Autoclave Method</li><li>Tube Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The high-pressure polyethylene (HDPE) market comprises primarily two production methods: the Autoclave Method and the Tube Method. The Autoclave Method involves polymerizing ethylene in a high-pressure reactor, leading to high-density polyethylene with robust properties suitable for packaging and piping. In contrast, the Tube Method utilizes a tubular reactor, allowing for continuous production. This method is efficient and yields low-density polyethylene, suitable for applications requiring flexibility. Both methods cater to diverse industrial needs, influencing the overall HDPE market dynamics.</p></p>
