<p><strong>The Automotive CMOS Clock Buffer Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Differential Clock Buffers</li><li>Single Ended Clock Buffers</li><li>Zero Delay Buffer</li><li>Fanout Buffers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive CMOS clock buffer market comprises several types, each catering to specific applications. Differential clock buffers transmit signals using two complementary outputs, enhancing noise immunity. Single-ended clock buffers offer simplicity and cost-effectiveness for basic applications. Zero delay buffers eliminate signal skew, ensuring precise timing, ideal for high-speed operations. Fanout buffers distribute a single clock signal to multiple outputs, maintaining signal integrity across systems. Together, these buffer types support reliable and efficient processing in automotive electronics.</p></p>
