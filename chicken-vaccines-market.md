<p><p>The chicken vaccines market is primarily categorized into live vaccines and killed vaccines. Live vaccines contain weakened or attenuated pathogens that stimulate an immune response without causing disease, providing strong and long-lasting immunity. Killed vaccines, on the other hand, consist of inactivated pathogens, prompting a safer immune response while often requiring multiple doses for effective protection. Both types are essential in preventing common avian diseases, thereby ensuring the health and productivity of poultry populations in the industry.</p></p>
