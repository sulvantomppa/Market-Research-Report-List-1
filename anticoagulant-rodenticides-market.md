<p><ul><li>FirstGenerationAnticoagulant</li><li>SecondGenerationAnticoagulant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The anticoagulant rodenticides market is segmented into two main types: first-generation and second-generation anticoagulants. First-generation anticoagulants, such as Warfarin, require multiple feeding sessions to be effective, as they allow rodents to build immunity. In contrast, second-generation anticoagulants, like Brodifacoum and Bromadiolone, are more potent and lethal, often requiring only a single feeding for effectiveness, thus leading to quicker results. Both types are widely used in pest control, catering to various rodent population management needs.</p></p>
