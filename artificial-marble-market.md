<p><p>The artificial marble market comprises primarily two key types: solid surface and engineered quartz stone. Solid surface materials are non-porous, customizable, and often used for countertops and sinks, providing a seamless appearance. Engineered quartz stone, on the other hand, is made from a blend of natural quartz crystals and resins, offering high durability and a wide range of colors and patterns. Both types cater to diverse aesthetic preferences and functional applications, making them popular choices in interior design and construction.</p></p>
